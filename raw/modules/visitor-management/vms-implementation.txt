Discovery questions to ask 
	•	Understanding the Current Process:
	•	Can you describe your current visitor management process?
	•	What are the primary challenges you face with your current system?
	•	
	•	 Identifying Specific Needs and Requirements:
	•	What specific features are you looking for in a visitor management solution?
	•	Are there any compliance or regulatory requirements your solution needs to adhere to 
	•	(e.g., data privacy laws, industry-specific regulations)?
	•	
	•	Volume and Type of Visitors:
	•	What is the average volume of visitors you receive daily/weekly?
	•	Do you have different types of visitors (e.g., contractors, clients, delivery personnel) that require different handling?
	•	
	•	Integration with Existing Systems:
	•	Do you have any existing systems (like access control, employee databases, etc.) that the visitor management solution needs to integrate with?
	•	How do you envision the integration of the visitor management solution with your current infrastructure?
	•	
	•	Security and Data Management:
	•	What are your security requirements for visitor data?
	•	How do you plan to store and manage the data collected from visitors?
	•	
	•	User Experience:
	•	What is the desired experience for your visitors when they arrive?
	•	How tech-savvy are your visitors and staff, and how might this impact their interaction with the system?
	•	
	•	Scalability and Flexibility:
	•	How do you anticipate your visitor management needs changing in the future?
	•	Are you looking for a solution that is scalable and adaptable to changing needs?
	•	
	•	Budget and Timeline:
	•	What is your budget for implementing a visitor management solution?
	•	What is your timeline for deployment?
	•	
	•	Reporting and Analytics:
	•	Are you looking for reporting or analytics capabilities in your visitor management system?
	•	What kind of data or insights are most important for your organization?
	•	
	•	Support and Training:
	•	What level of support and training will be required for your staff?
	•	How do you plan to handle ongoing maintenance and updates to the system?

Implementation Questions:
	•	Do they see traffic from invited visitors, walk-in visitors or both?
	•	Who does the visitor check in? Is it the security guard or receptionist, both or self-checkin?
	•	When the invite is sent to the visitor or for a walk-in visitor - what information do they want from the visitor/the visitor to enter? I.e Identity Proof upload, How many days they are visiting, License plate number of the vehicle, if the visitor requires accommodation, etc
	•	What information will the receptionist/security need to check when a visitor comes to the office? i.e NDA, Identification, belongings, etc. 
	•	Are there any resources that need to be given to a visitor? Ex - Parking? We do not support desks and meals currently. 
	•	What is the notification flow expected from the host, the front desk, and the security? Ex - If a visitor checks in, should there be an email notification sent to the host
Terms to Keep in Mind:
	•	Host → Employee/User creating the invitation
	•	Scheduled visitor → Invited Visitor
	•	Visitor Registration Flow → Process where an invited visitor uploads data or fills in necessary information before visit
	•	Digipass → QR code given to an invited visitor for faster check-in
	•	Front Desk Dashboard → Dashboard for front desk/receptionist
	•	Invite form → where the host creates an invitation
	•	Invite Dashboard → List of invites on Invite Visitor tab

Using the Internal Workbench Tool to Assign RBAC Privileges
Rather than raising an SE ticket to enable RBAC permissions, you can use the internal RBAC Access Viewer tool to assign role-based privileges directly.
Tool URL: https://mis-workbench.moveinsync.com/
Steps to assign a privilege to a role:
	•	Log in to the workbench using your MoveInSync credentials
	•	Click on RBAC Access Viewer
	•	Select the Environment the client site is on — Stage, Singapore, Europe, or India (Mumbai)
	•	Select the Business Unit ID (BUID) of the client
	•	Select the Role you want to configure (e.g. RECEPTIONIST, VISITOR_SECURITY_GUARD, or any custom role)
	•	The permission matrix for that role will load — find the relevant privilege (refer to the RBAC properties table above)
	•	Assign the required access level — Read or Edit — and save
You can also create a new role or a new privilege directly from the tool using the + Create Role and + Create Privilege buttons in the top right.
Note: Only users with access to the workbench can perform this action. If you do not have access, raise a request with Yogesh or Anshul to get access.

Walk-in Visitor Flow
Step-by-Step Flow (Regular OTP - Default)
Step 1 - Select Visitor Type

FD clicks "Walk-in Visitor" on the Front Desk Dashboard
Modal opens with a dropdown to select visitor type (e.g. Business Guests, Contract Employees, Delivery Personnel)
Visitor types are configurable - raise SE to update the list


Step 2 - Verify Visitor

FD selects verification method: Verify via Email or Verify via Phone Number
OTP is sent to the visitor; visitor shares it with the FD
Visitor's profile fields (Name, Email, Phone) are auto-populated once verified


Step 3 - Enter Visitor Details


FD fills in visitor information across two screens:

Screen 1: Name, Email, Phone Number (pre-filled from OTP step)
Screen 2: Host Name (mandatory), Office (mandatory), Duration (mandatory), + additional fields


Additional fields visible on Screen 2 depend on visitor type configuration:

Company, Company Address, Designation, Purpose of Visit, Location - Coming From
Each field can be enabled/disabled and set as optional or mandatory per visitor type
Raise SE to configure which fields show and their mandatory/optional state


Step 4 - Check-in


On Save, visitor is checked in
Relevant notifications go to host (email / MS Teams if configured)

Invite Visitor Flow
Step 1 - Initiate Invite
The host navigates to Visitor Management → Invite Visitor and clicks Create Invite.
The Invite form opens with the following sections:
	•	Invite Details (left panel)
	•	Visitor Selection (right panel)
Step 2 - Fill Invite Details
The host is required to enter:
	•	Inviting On
	•	My Behalf
	•	Someone else’s behalf (if allowBookingsForOthers is enabled)
	•	Title (mandatory)
	•	Type of Visit (mandatory)
	•	Configurable list (e.g., Business Guests, Contractors, etc.)
	•	Office (mandatory)
	•	Auto-populated based on host’s default office
	•	Editable based on configuration
	•	Start Date & End Date (mandatory)
	•	Start Time & End Time (mandatory)
	•	Repeat Booking
	•	Supports recurring/multi-day invites
Step 3 — Add Visitors
On the right panel, visitors can be added via:
	•	Search existing visitor (by name/email/phone)
	•	+ New Guest (manual entry)
	•	Bulk Upload (if Guest_Bulk_Upload is enabled)
Each visitor includes:
	•	Name
	•	Email
	•	Phone Number
	•	Additional fields (based on configuration)

Step 4 - Additional Inputs (Optional)
Based on configuration, the host may also:
	•	Add Personal Note to Guest
	•	Add Note to Admins (notesToAdmins)
	•	Add Note to Front Desk (noteToFrontDesk)

	•	Assign parking (if enabled — currently limited support)
	•	Fill custom fields

	•	Invite is created for each visitor
